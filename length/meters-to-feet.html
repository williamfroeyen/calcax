Meters (m)

Feet (ft)

To convert meters to feet (m to ft), multiply the number of meters by 3.2808. Therefore, 1 meter equals 3.2808 feet. The foot is an ancient unit of length that has been used in many parts of the world, including Norway. It is based on the length of an average human foot, but the exact length has varied throughout history and between different countries.

In Norway, the foot was abolished as an official unit of measurement when the metric system was introduced in 1875. However, it is still used in some contexts, such as in the construction industry and among boat enthusiasts. Today, a foot is defined as 30.48 centimeters or 12 inches. This is the international standard for the foot, and it applies in both the United States and the United Kingdom. It is important to note that there are some minor variations in the length of a foot, depending on where it is used. For example, the U.S. "survey foot" is approximately 0.3048006 meters, which is slightly longer than the international foot.

The foot remains a practical unit of measurement in certain industries, such as aviation, where altitude is often measured in feet. It is also commonly used in everyday situations, such as measuring height or room dimensions in countries that have not fully adopted the metric system.
