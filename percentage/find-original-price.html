To calculate the original price of a discounted product, use the following formula: Original Price = Current Price / (1 - (Discount Percentage / 100)). For example, if a sweater costs $420 and is on sale with a 30% discount, first calculate 30 / 100, which equals 0.30. Then, subtract 0.30 from 1, resulting in 0.70. Finally, divide 420 by 0.70 to find the original price of $600.

Quickly calculate the original price: Divide the discount percentage by 100 to get the discount factor. For instance, a 40% discount becomes a discount factor of 0.40. To find the original price, divide the current price by 1 - discount factor. With a 40% discount, think of 1 - 0.40 as 0.60. Then, divide the current price by 0.60 to find the original price. If you can quickly calculate the discount factor and subtract it from 1 mentally, you won't need to enter 1 - (discount percentage / 100) into the calculator.

This tool is also known as a pre-sale price calculator, discount reverse calculator, or a markdown calculator. Each of these terms refers to the process of finding the original price before a discount was applied. Knowing the pre-discount price can be very useful for budgeting, price comparison, and general financial planning.

Understanding how discounts affect the actual price of goods can help you make more informed purchasing decisions. It allows you to see the real value of the savings and avoid being misled by seemingly large discounts on overpriced items.
