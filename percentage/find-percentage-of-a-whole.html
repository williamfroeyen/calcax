To find out how much a certain percentage represents of the whole number, multiply the percentage by the whole number, and then divide by 100. The formula is: Percentage Value = (Percentage * Whole Number) / 100. For example, if you want to find 60% of $200, first multiply 60 by 200, which equals 12,000. Then, divide 12,000 by 100, which results in 120. Thus, 60% of $200 is $120.

There's also another method that's especially useful if you're using a calculator and want to be quick. Mentally, you can convert the percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100, or moving the decimal point two places to the left. 65% becomes 0.65, and 6% becomes 0.06. This is called a percentage factor. Then, multiply the percentage factor by the whole number to get the percentage value. To calculate 60% of $200, mentally convert 60% to 0.60. Then, multiply 0.60 by 200 on the calculator, and you get 120.
