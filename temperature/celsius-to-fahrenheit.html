To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit, first multiply the Celsius degrees by 9/5 or 1.8. Then, add 32 to the result to get the number of degrees in Fahrenheit. The formula is: °F = (°C * 9/5) + 32. Fahrenheit is a temperature scale developed by the German physicist Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit in 1724. The scale is primarily used in the United States and some Caribbean countries, while the rest of the world mainly uses Celsius.

On the Fahrenheit scale, the freezing point of water is set to 32 degrees (°F) and the boiling point to 212 °F, based on experiments Fahrenheit conducted with water, ice, and salt solutions. The scale is divided into 180 degrees between these two points, in contrast to Celsius, which has a division of 100 degrees. Originally, Fahrenheit defined his zero point (0 °F) based on the coldest temperature he could achieve with a mixture of ice, water, and ammonium chloride. He then set 96 °F as the human body temperature, although modern measurements have adjusted this to approximately 98.6 °F. The Fahrenheit scale is commonly used in everyday speech in countries where it is standard, especially for weather forecasts and temperature measurements in households and industries.
