The US fluid ounce, often abbreviated as "fl oz" or "oz fl," is an American unit of volume primarily used for measuring liquids. It is one of the traditional units still widely used in the United States, especially in grocery stores, cooking, and industrial applications. One US fluid ounce is exactly equivalent to 29.5735 milliliters (ml).

The origin of the fluid ounce can be traced back to the British Empire, where various systems for weight and volume were used. The American version of the fluid ounce differs from the British (imperial fluid ounce), which is slightly larger and equals approximately 28.413 ml. This difference is due to historical developments in the standardization of measurement units after the United States and Britain went separate ways in the 19th century.

The US fluid ounce is used in various contexts, including cooking and recipes where liquid quantities such as milk, oil, and water are often measured in fluid ounces. Beverages like soda, juice, and other liquids in the United States are often labeled with volume in fluid ounces. In the pharmaceutical industry, the fluid ounce is also used for dosing liquid medications.
