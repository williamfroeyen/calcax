To convert gallons to liters, multiply the number of gallons by 3.78541178. The formula is: Liters = gallons * 3.78541178. Thus, 1 gallon equals 3.78541178 liters.

The liter is a unit of volume measurement used in many contexts, both in everyday life and in scientific settings. A liter is often abbreviated as "L" and corresponds to the volume of a cubic decimeter (1 dm³). This means that a liter holds the same amount as a cube with sides of 10 centimeters. Liters are one of the most commonly used units for measuring liquids, such as water, milk, juice, and fuel.

A liter is often divided into smaller units such as deciliters (dl), centiliters (cl), and milliliters (ml). The relationship between these units is based on the decimal system, making it easy to convert between them. For example, 1 liter is equal to 10 deciliters, 100 centiliters, or 1000 milliliters. This system is particularly practical in cooking, where recipes often use different volume measurements for ingredients.

In the International System of Units (SI), the liter is a non-SI unit that is still accepted for use alongside SI units. The SI unit for volume is actually the cubic meter (m³), but the liter is often used because it is more practical in many situations. For example, it is more common to say that a milk carton contains 1 liter of milk instead of 0.001 cubic meters.
