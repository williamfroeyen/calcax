To convert from liters to gallons, multiply the number of liters by 0.264172052. The formula is: Gallons = liters * 0.264172052. Thus, 1 liter equals 0.264172052 gallons. There are three different versions of the gallon. The US gallon is most often meant when someone says gallon without further specification.

The US gallon is a unit of volume measurement primarily used in the United States and a few other countries. One US gallon is equivalent to 3.78541 liters. This differs from the British (imperial) gallon, which is larger and equals 4.54609 liters. The difference between these two units can often cause confusion, especially in international contexts where both may occur.

The US gallon is mainly used for measuring liquids such as fuel, milk, water, and other liquids in daily life. For example, gasoline prices in the US are usually quoted per gallon, which is an important part of everyday economics for motorists. In contrast, most countries in the world use the liter system, which is part of the international SI system.

Historically, the US gallon originates from the English system of measurement and weight brought to North America by British colonists. Over time, the United States developed its own standards, resulting in today's US gallon. This happened while Britain transitioned to the imperial system in 1824, which standardized the British gallon at a larger volume.

There are also smaller subunits associated with the US gallon. One gallon is divided into 4 quarts, one quart into 2 pints, one pint into 2 cups, and one cup into 8 fluid ounces. This system is practical in many American recipes and in household use, although it may seem complicated to those accustomed to the decimal system in metric measurements.
