Convert Grams to Ounces

Grams (g)

Ounces (oz)

To convert from grams to ounces, multiply the number of grams by 0.0352739619. The formula is: Ounces = grams * 0.0352739619. The ounce is an old unit of measurement still used today, especially in English-speaking countries like the United States and the United Kingdom. The word "ounce" comes from the Latin word "uncia," meaning one-twelfth, which makes sense as an ounce was originally defined as one-twelfth of a pound. There are two main types of ounces: fluid ounce and avoirdupois ounce, and they are used for different purposes.

An avoirdupois ounce is the most commonly used variant and is approximately equal to 28.35 grams. This type of ounce is mainly used to measure the weight of solids such as food, jewelry, and metals. For example, a chocolate bar might weigh around 3.5 ounces, which is about 100 grams.

A fluid ounce, on the other hand, is used to measure the volume of liquids. In the US system, a fluid ounce is approximately equal to 29.57 milliliters, while the British (imperial) fluid ounce is slightly larger and is approximately equal to 28.41 milliliters. This can create confusion when reading recipes or measuring liquids in international contexts.

The ounce is also used in specific industries. In the gold and precious metals trade, it is common to use the troy ounce, which weighs approximately 31.1 grams. The troy ounce has its roots from medieval trading cities in Europe and is still the standard for measuring precious metals such as gold, silver, and platinum.
